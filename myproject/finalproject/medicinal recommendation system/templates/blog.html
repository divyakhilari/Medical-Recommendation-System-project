🚀 Building a Symptom-Based Disease Diagnosis Web App

Developed by Divya & Shruti | FYMCA Students

The Inspiration

As FYMCA students passionate about Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, we wanted to create something meaningful that connects technology with healthcare. Many people struggle to understand their symptoms, and we envisioned a tool that could provide quick, informative insights for both patients and doctors.

The Problem

People often experience overlapping symptoms, making it difficult to differentiate between diseases. Our challenge was to design a solution that simplifies this process and empowers individuals with accessible health information, while also supporting doctors in analyzing patient data.

The Solution

We developed a web app using Flask for the backend and a Decision Tree Classifier model for predictions. Here’s how it works:

  1. Symptom Input: Users enter their symptoms through a simple, user-friendly interface.
  2. Machine Learning Model: Our Decision Tree Classifier, trained on a dataset of symptoms and diseases, predicts possible conditions.
  3. Prediction: The app instantly suggests the most likely disease.
  4. Doctor’s Aid: Doctors can use the app’s predictions and detailed information to support their own analysis and decision-making.
  5. Additional Information: Users also receive details about the disease, precautions, medications, diet, and workout tips.

Key Features

  • User-Friendly Interface: Designed for simplicity and ease of use.
  • Accurate Predictions: Powered by machine learning for reliable results.
  • Doctor-Friendly Insights: Helps healthcare professionals analyze symptoms and cross-check diagnoses.
  • Comprehensive Information: Includes disease descriptions, precautions, medications, diet, and fitness tips.
  • Educational Content: Blog posts and resources to spread health awareness.

Conclusion

Our Symptom-Based Disease Diagnosis Web App reflects our journey as students exploring AI and healthcare. It’s more than a project—it’s a step toward making healthcare information accessible, personalized, and user-centric. By supporting both patients and doctors, we hope this initiative inspires others to combine technology with compassion for a healthier future.
